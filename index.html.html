History of Cinema

1895 - The Lumière Brothers

On December 28, 1895, in Paris, the Lumière brothers projected short films for the first time to a paying audience. "La sortie de l'usine" marked the official beginning of cinema as entertainment.

Precursors: Magic Lantern

From the 17th century, the magic lantern projected painted images to entertain and educate. It was a key step towards the illusion of movement.

1902 - Méliès and effects

Georges Méliès revolutionized cinema with "A Trip to the Moon," introducing tricks, models, and creative editing that inspire modern cinema.

Silent Era - Large Cameras

In the 1920s, enormous cameras like the Mitchell BNC captured silent films. They were heavy, but they produced stunning images.
